Career Options After SSC

Arts

Arts Stream Overview

The Arts Stream offers a rich and diverse curriculum that encompasses a wide range of subjects, focusing on humanities, social sciences, and creative arts. It is ideal for students with an interest in literature, history, sociology, psychology, languages, and the arts. The stream fosters creativity, critical thinking, and communication skills.

2. Subjects in Arts Stream

The Arts Stream provides a wide array of subjects, offering students the flexibility to pursue areas of interest. The subjects are categorized as follows:

a) Core Subjects:
  1. English: A compulsory language subject focusing on communication, writing skills, and critical analysis of literature.
  2. History: Study of past events, civilizations, and their impact on the present and future.
  3. Political Science: Study of political systems, government structures, and global political dynamics.
  4. Sociology: Study of society, social behavior, and societal structures.
b) Elective Subjects (optional):
  1. Psychology: Study of the human mind, behavior, and mental processes.
  2. Geography: Study of Earth's physical features, climates, and the relationship between humans and their environment.
  3. Philosophy: Study of fundamental questions regarding existence, knowledge, values, and reason.
  4. Fine Arts: Involves creative disciplines like painting, sculpture, and visual arts.
3. Career Opportunities after Arts Stream

The Arts Stream opens up diverse career paths in areas related to humanities, social sciences, and creativity. Some potential careers include:

  1. Teaching & Academia
  2. Journalism & Media
  3. Psychology & Counseling
  4. Social Work & NGOs
  5. Political Analysis
  6. Public Relations
  7. Advertising & Marketing
  8. Graphic Design & Fine Arts
4. Eligibility Criteria
  • Minimum Marks in Class 10: Students need to have passed Class 10 with a minimum of 50-60% marks, depending on the institution.
  • Subjects Taken in Class 10: Students should have studied subjects like English, Social Studies, and any elective subjects offered.
5. Potential Career Paths for Arts Students
After Completing Class 12 Arts:
  • Teaching: Pursue degrees like B.Ed for teaching history, political science, or languages.
  • Journalism: Opt for courses in Journalism or Mass Communication for a career in media.
  • Psychology: Enroll in B.A. or B.Sc. Psychology for careers in counseling or therapy.
  • Social Work: Choose degrees in Social Work for careers in NGOs or community development.
Postgraduate Studies Options:
  • M.A. in Psychology
  • M.A. in Political Science or Sociology
  • M.A. in Journalism or Mass Communication
Competitive Exams:
  • UPSC: For Civil Services, Indian Administrative Service (IAS), or Indian Foreign Services (IFS).
  • CAT/MAT: For MBA programs in various management institutes.
  • CLAT: For Law admissions in various colleges.
  • NET: For teaching and research positions in universities.

Commmerce

Commerce Stream Overview

The Commerce Stream is one of the most popular educational streams that focuses on the study of trade, business, finance, and economics. It is ideal for students who are interested in pursuing careers in accountancy, business administration, finance, economics, and entrepreneurship. The stream emphasizes analytical thinking, business acumen, and management skills.

2. Subjects in Commerce Stream

The Commerce Stream includes a combination of core and elective subjects, offering students the flexibility to shape their careers according to their interests. The subjects are divided into the following categories:

a) Core Subjects:
  1. Accountancy: Study of financial transactions, bookkeeping, and preparation of financial statements.
  2. Economics: Focus on microeconomics, macroeconomics, and the functioning of economies.
  3. Business Studies: Study of business organization, management principles, and entrepreneurship.
  4. English: A compulsory language subject focusing on communication skills and critical thinking.
b) Elective Subjects (optional):
  1. Mathematics: Essential for students interested in careers in finance, analytics, and economics.
  2. Informatics Practices (IP): Introduction to computer programming, data handling, and database management.
  3. Entrepreneurship: Study of business startups, innovation, and risk management.
  4. Physical Education: Focus on fitness, sports science, and overall well-being.
3. Career Opportunities after Commerce Stream

The Commerce Stream opens up various career paths for students, including:

  1. Chartered Accountancy (CA)
  2. Company Secretary (CS)
  3. Banking & Finance
  4. Business Management
  5. Stock Market Analyst
  6. Data Analytics
  7. Law (Corporate Law or General Law)
  8. Teaching & Academia
4. Eligibility Criteria
  • Minimum Marks in Class 10: Students need to have passed Class 10 with a minimum of 50-60% marks, depending on the institution.
  • Subjects Taken in Class 10: Students should have studied Mathematics (optional for some boards), English, and Social Studies.
5. Potential Career Paths for Commerce Students
After Completing Class 12 Commerce:
  • Accountancy: Pursue courses like CA, CMA, or CPA for careers in accounting and auditing.
  • Business Administration: Enroll in BBA or BBM programs for managerial roles in businesses.
  • Finance & Banking: Opt for degrees like B.Com, Bachelor of Banking and Insurance (BBI), or Bachelor of Financial Markets (BFM).
  • Economics: Pursue a Bachelorâ€™s in Economics for careers in policymaking, research, or analysis.
Postgraduate Studies Options:
  • M.Com (Master of Commerce)
  • MBA (Master of Business Administration)
  • LLB (For Law careers)
Competitive Exams:
  • CPT/IPCC: For Chartered Accountancy.
  • CLAT: For Law admissions.
  • CAT/MAT: For MBA programs.
  • SSC & Banking Exams: For government and banking sector jobs.

Science

Science Stream Overview

The Science Stream is one of the most sought-after educational streams, offering a variety of subjects that help students understand the world around them through analytical thinking, reasoning, and experimentation. The stream is popular among students who are keen on pursuing careers in engineering, medicine, research, and technology.

2. Subjects in Science Stream

The Science Stream consists of a combination of core and elective subjects, depending on the student's choice. The major subjects are divided into two categories:

a) Core Subjects:
  1. Physics: The study of matter, energy, and the interactions between them.
  2. Chemistry: The science of substances, their properties, reactions, and how they change.
  3. Mathematics: The study of numbers, shapes, and patterns, essential for engineering, technology, and various fields.
  4. Biology: The study of living organisms, their structure, function, and the relationships between them.
b) Elective Subjects (optional):
  1. Computer Science: Introduction to computer programming, data structures, algorithms, and software development.
  2. Environmental Science: Study of environmental issues, sustainability, and conservation.
  3. Geography: Study of Earth's landscapes, environments, and the relationships between people and their environments.
  4. Physical Education: Focus on fitness, sports science, and physical well-being.
3. Career Opportunities after Science Stream

The Science Stream opens up a wide range of career opportunities. Some of the potential careers include:

  1. Engineering
  2. Medicine & Healthcare
  3. Science & Research
  4. Technology & IT
  5. Environmental Sciences
  6. Teaching & Academia
4. Eligibility Criteria
  • Minimum Marks in Class 10: Students need to have passed their Class 10 with a minimum of 50-60% marks, depending on the board and institution.
  • Subjects Taken in Class 10: Students should have studied subjects like Mathematics, Science (Physics, Chemistry, Biology), and English.
5. Potential Career Paths for Science Students
After Completing Class 12 Science:
  • Engineering: Students can pursue engineering degrees in various disciplines.
  • Medical Science: A popular choice for students pursuing MBBS, BDS, Nursing, or other medical degrees.
  • Computer Science: Pursue degrees like BCA, BSc Computer Science, or IT-related degrees.
  • Pure Sciences: Students can opt for B.Sc. in Physics, Chemistry, or Biology for research-focused careers.
Postgraduate Studies Options:
  • M.Tech (for Engineering students)
  • MD (for Medical students)
  • M.Sc (for Science students)
Competitive Exams:
  • JEE Main/Advanced: For Engineering
  • NEET: For Medicine
  • GATE: For Postgraduate Engineering Course
  • CSIR-NET: For Research in Science

Diploma

Diploma Stream Overview

The Diploma Stream offers practical, hands-on training for students who want to enter the workforce quickly with specialized skills. It provides courses in various technical fields like engineering, hospitality, design, and healthcare. A diploma program is often a great alternative for students who wish to gain expertise in a specific domain without the commitment of a full bachelor's degree.

2. Subjects in Diploma Stream

Diploma programs vary depending on the field of study. The subjects are categorized based on the specific diploma stream chosen:

a) Core Subjects (based on specialization):
  1. Diploma in Engineering: Subjects like Applied Mathematics, Engineering Mechanics, Engineering Drawing, Electrical Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Civil Engineering, and Computer Science.
  2. Diploma in Hospitality: Subjects like Front Office Management, Food Production, Housekeeping, and Event Management.
  3. Diploma in Design: Subjects like Fashion Design, Graphic Design, Textile Design, and Interior Designing.
  4. Diploma in Healthcare: Subjects like Medical Terminology, Anatomy, Physiology, Patient Care, and Nursing.
3. Career Opportunities after Diploma Stream

Diploma programs provide students with practical skills that open up a wide range of career opportunities. Some potential career paths include:

  1. Engineering Technician
  2. Mechanical Technician
  3. Civil Engineer Assistant
  4. Graphic Designer
  5. Fashion Designer
  6. Hotel Management Professional
  7. Healthcare Assistant
  8. Event Manager
4. Eligibility Criteria
  • Minimum Marks in Class 10: Students need to have passed Class 10 with a minimum of 50-60% marks, depending on the institution and the specific diploma program.
  • Subjects Taken in Class 10: Students should have studied subjects like Mathematics, Science, and English for most technical diploma courses.
5. Potential Career Paths for Diploma Students
After Completing a Diploma:
  • Engineering Technicians: Diploma holders can work as technical assistants in various engineering fields.
  • Healthcare Professionals: Diploma holders can work in hospitals, clinics, or healthcare facilities as assistants or technicians.
  • Hospitality Industry: A diploma in hospitality can lead to careers in hotel management, catering, and event management.
  • Design and Fashion: Diploma holders in design or fashion can pursue careers in graphic design, fashion design, and related fields.
Postgraduate Studies Options:
  • After completing a diploma, students can opt for a Bachelor's degree in the same or related field (e.g., B.Tech, BBA, BSc in relevant domains).
Competitive Exams:
  • Entrance Exams for Degree Courses: Students may need to appear for entrance exams for admission to degree programs after completing their diploma.
  • State-Level Exams: Various states conduct entrance exams for admission into diploma programs and further studies.

Others

Other Courses After 10th Overview

After completing Class 10, students can explore a wide range of professional courses that prepare them for specific careers. These courses cater to different interests and skills, ranging from technical training to creative arts. These courses usually involve hands-on learning, industry exposure, and opportunities to develop expertise in specialized fields.

1. Popular Professional Courses After 10th

Students can choose from various professional courses, such as:

a) Engineering Diploma

Engineering Diploma courses are available in various streams like Civil, Mechanical, Electrical, and Computer Science. These programs offer practical skills and can be a stepping stone for students who wish to pursue a B.Tech/B.E. later.

b) ITI (Industrial Training Institutes)

ITI offers vocational training in fields like welding, electrician work, fitter, plumbing, and more. This course provides hands-on technical skills and prepares students for careers in industrial and manual labor sectors.

c) Animation, VFX, and Multimedia

Students with an interest in digital arts can pursue professional courses in Animation, VFX, and Multimedia. These programs focus on creating visual effects, 2D/3D animation, and multimedia production, leading to careers in gaming, films, and advertising.

d) Fashion Design

For creative individuals, fashion design courses offer skills in designing clothing, textiles, and accessories. Students can explore careers in fashion houses, retail brands, and the entertainment industry.

e) Event Management

Event Management courses teach students how to plan and manage events, conferences, exhibitions, and weddings. This industry is ideal for those with strong organizational and communication skills.

f) Hotel Management

Hotel Management programs prepare students for roles in the hospitality industry. Courses cover hotel operations, front office management, catering, and customer service, leading to careers in hotels, resorts, and restaurants.

g) Travel and Tourism

Travel and Tourism courses introduce students to the tourism industry, including travel planning, tour guiding, hospitality, and tourism marketing. Graduates can work in travel agencies, airlines, and tour companies.

h) Journalism and Mass Communication

Journalism and Mass Communication courses prepare students for careers in media, broadcasting, news reporting, and public relations. It is suitable for individuals with strong communication and writing skills.

i) Commercial Pilot Training

For students passionate about aviation, commercial pilot training offers the opportunity to pursue a career as a pilot. This course covers the technical and practical aspects of flying, navigation, and aviation regulations.

2. Career Opportunities after Professional Courses

Upon completion of these courses, students can explore several career opportunities, including:

  1. Diploma Engineer
  2. ITI Technician
  3. Animator/Graphic Designer
  4. Fashion Designer
  5. Event Manager
  6. Hotel Manager
  7. Tourism and Travel Specialist
  8. Journalist/Reporter
  9. Commercial Pilot
3. Eligibility Criteria
  • Minimum Marks in Class 10: Students should have passed Class 10 with a minimum of 50-60% marks, depending on the course.
  • Subject Requirements: Specific courses may require certain subjects like Mathematics, Science, or English in Class 10.
4. Competitive Exams for Professional Courses
  • Polytechnic Entrance Exams (for Diploma Courses)
  • ITI Entrance Exams

Bachelor's in Arts

Bachelor of Arts (B.A.) Stream Overview

A Bachelor of Arts (B.A.) degree offers a broad range of subjects in the humanities, social sciences, and liberal arts. This program emphasizes critical thinking, analytical skills, and intellectual growth. Students can specialize in various disciplines like literature, history, sociology, psychology, political science, philosophy, and more. A B.A. degree is often the first step for students pursuing careers in education, media, law, public administration, and creative industries.

2. Subjects in Bachelor of Arts (B.A.)

The B.A. degree offers a variety of subjects, enabling students to pursue their interests in various fields. The subjects are broadly categorized into core and elective areas:

a) Core Subjects:
  1. English Literature: The study of English literature, including classical and modern works, poetry, drama, and prose.
  2. History: The study of historical events, cultures, and societies, examining how past events shaped the present.
  3. Political Science: The study of political systems, governments, and the role of individuals and institutions in society.
  4. Philosophy: The study of fundamental questions regarding existence, knowledge, ethics, and reason.
  5. Sociology: The study of society, social behavior, and the dynamics of social groups, institutions, and structures.
b) Elective Subjects (optional):
  1. Psychology: The study of the human mind, behavior, emotions, cognition, and mental processes.
  2. Economics: The study of how societies allocate resources, the functioning of markets, and economic policies.
  3. Geography: The study of Earth's landscapes, environments, and the relationships between people and their surroundings.
  4. Fine Arts: The study of visual arts, including painting, sculpture, and other forms of creative expression.
  5. Journalism: The study of media, communication, news writing, and reporting.
3. Career Opportunities after B.A.

A B.A. degree opens up numerous career opportunities across various fields. Some of the potential careers include:

  1. Teacher/Professor
  2. Journalist/Writer
  3. Political Analyst
  4. Social Worker
  5. HR Manager
  6. Advertising/PR Specialist
  7. Content Creator
  8. Government Jobs (UPSC, Civil Services)
4. Eligibility Criteria
  • Minimum Marks in Class 12: Students need to have passed their Class 12 with a minimum of 50-60% marks, depending on the institution.
  • Subjects Taken in Class 12: Students should have studied relevant subjects like Humanities, Social Sciences, or Languages in Class 12.
5. Potential Career Paths for B.A. Students
After Completing Class 12 and B.A.:
  • Teaching: Students can become school teachers or university professors by pursuing a B.Ed. or higher education.
  • Media and Journalism: Graduates can work as journalists, editors, or media professionals in print, digital, or broadcast media.
  • Social Services: Students can pursue careers in NGOs, social work, or public administration.
  • Public Relations/Advertising: Graduates can work in advertising, marketing, or public relations firms.
  • Government Jobs: A B.A. degree opens doors for civil services and other government roles through competitive exams (UPSC, SSC, etc.).
Postgraduate Studies Options:
  • MA (Master of Arts in specialized fields like Political Science, Psychology, Sociology, etc.)
  • MBA (for students interested in business and management)
  • LLB (for students pursuing a career in law)
  • MSW (Master of Social Work for students interested in social services)
Competitive Exams:
  • UPSC Civil Services Exam: For government and administrative roles
  • CAT: For MBA admissions
  • NET (National Eligibility Test): For university-level teaching
  • CLAT: For law school admissions

Bachelor of Commerce (B.Com.) Stream Overview

The Bachelor of Commerce (B.Com.) is one of the most popular undergraduate degrees pursued by students interested in business, finance, economics, and accounting. The B.Com. program provides students with a deep understanding of financial management, taxation, business law, economics, and more. It is an ideal foundation for those looking to enter the business world, pursue higher education in commerce, or prepare for professional certifications in accounting and finance.

2. Subjects in Bachelor of Commerce (B.Com.)

The B.Com. degree offers a range of subjects to equip students with the knowledge necessary for various career paths in commerce. The subjects are divided into core and elective areas:

a) Core Subjects:
  1. Accounting: The study of financial transactions, balance sheets, and profit and loss statements.
  2. Business Law: The study of legal principles and regulations governing businesses and commercial transactions.
  3. Economics: The study of economic principles, including microeconomics, macroeconomics, and international trade.
  4. Business Communication: The study of effective communication techniques used in business settings.
  5. Statistics: The study of data collection, analysis, and interpretation, particularly in the context of business and economics.
b) Elective Subjects (optional):
  1. Taxation: The study of tax laws, tax planning, and strategies for businesses and individuals.
  2. Auditing: The study of the examination of financial records to ensure accuracy and compliance.
  3. Banking and Insurance: The study of financial institutions, insurance products, and banking services.
  4. Entrepreneurship: The study of starting and managing new business ventures.
  5. Corporate Finance: The study of financial management in large corporations, including capital structure and investment decisions.
3. Career Opportunities after B.Com.

A B.Com. degree opens doors to numerous career opportunities in the fields of finance, accounting, banking, and business. Some of the potential careers include:

  1. Chartered Accountant (CA)
  2. Company Secretary (CS)
  3. Cost Accountant
  4. Banking and Financial Services
  5. Insurance Analyst
  6. Auditor
  7. Tax Consultant
  8. Entrepreneur
4. Eligibility Criteria
  • Minimum Marks in Class 12: Students need to have passed their Class 12 with a minimum of 50-60% marks, depending on the institution.
  • Subjects Taken in Class 12: Students should have studied Commerce or similar subjects in Class 12.
5. Potential Career Paths for B.Com. Students
After Completing Class 12 and B.Com.:
  • Chartered Accountant (CA): Students can pursue CA to specialize in accounting and taxation.
  • Company Secretary (CS): A professional qualification to manage corporate governance and legal affairs.
  • Banking and Financial Services: Graduates can enter the banking sector in roles like investment banking, financial analysis, or risk management.
  • Auditing and Taxation: B.Com. graduates can work as auditors, tax consultants, or financial advisors.
  • Entrepreneurship: B.Com. graduates with an entrepreneurial mindset can start their own businesses or consult on business operations.
Postgraduate Studies Options:
  • M.Com (Master of Commerce)
  • MBA (Master of Business Administration) for a career in management
  • PGDM (Post Graduate Diploma in Management)
  • CA (Chartered Accountancy)
  • CS (Company Secretaryship)
Competitive Exams:
  • CA Foundation Exam: For Chartered Accountancy aspirants
  • CS Foundation Exam: For Company Secretary aspirants
  • CAT: For MBA admissions
  • UPSC: For various civil services and government roles

IT fields

IT Career Options after HSC

Information Technology (IT) is one of the fastest-growing sectors, offering a variety of career opportunities for students after completing their HSC (12th Standard). With advancements in technology, IT professionals play a crucial role in shaping industries across the globe. Students interested in coding, networking, cybersecurity, and emerging technologies can explore various IT-focused courses and career paths.

2. IT Courses after HSC

Students can pursue a variety of IT-related courses based on their interests and career goals. Some popular options include:

a) Bachelor of Computer Applications (BCA)

A three-year undergraduate program focused on programming languages, software development, and computer systems. BCA graduates can work as software developers, web designers, or system analysts.

b) B.Sc. in Information Technology (B.Sc. IT)

This course emphasizes IT fundamentals, including programming, networking, databases, and cybersecurity. Graduates can pursue careers in IT management, database administration, or technical support.

c) Diploma in IT

A shorter and specialized program covering core IT skills, such as software engineering, networking, and web development. This option is ideal for students looking to enter the IT workforce quickly.

d) Web Development and Design Courses

These courses focus on building websites and web applications. Students learn HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and frameworks like Angular or React. Careers include Web Developer or Front-End Developer.

e) Game Design and Development

Students interested in gaming can opt for this course, which teaches game programming, design, and 3D modeling. Graduates can work in the gaming and animation industries.

f) Certification in Ethical Hacking and Cybersecurity

A certification course focusing on network security, ethical hacking, and protecting systems from cyber threats. Professionals can work as Cybersecurity Analysts or Ethical Hackers.

g) Cloud Computing and DevOps

Specialized courses in cloud platforms like AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud, combined with DevOps practices. Graduates can become Cloud Engineers or DevOps Specialists.

h) Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning (AI & ML)

AI & ML courses teach students about intelligent systems, data processing, and predictive modeling. Graduates can work in fields like AI development, data analysis, and robotics.

3. Career Opportunities in IT

After completing IT-related courses, students can pursue diverse career opportunities, such as:

  1. Software Developer
  2. Web Designer/Developer
  3. IT Support Specialist
  4. Database Administrator
  5. Cybersecurity Analyst
  6. Cloud Engineer
  7. Game Developer
  8. AI/ML Engineer
  9. Network Administrator
4. Eligibility Criteria
  • Minimum Marks: Students must have passed their HSC with at least 50% marks.
  • Subject Requirements: Most IT courses require Mathematics as a subject in HSC.
  • Entrance Exams: Some institutes conduct entrance tests for admission to IT programs.
5. Advantages of Pursuing IT Careers
  • High demand for IT professionals across industries.
  • Opportunities for global employment and remote work.
  • Dynamic field with constant technological advancements.
  • Lucrative salary packages and career growth potential.

Competitive Exams

Competitive Exams after Graduation

Competitive exams after graduation are gateways to prestigious job roles in the public and private sectors or advanced education opportunities. These exams test candidates on various skills such as reasoning, quantitative aptitude, subject knowledge, and general awareness. They are highly sought after for career growth, government jobs, and academic pursuits.

2. Popular Competitive Exams

Graduates from various disciplines can choose from numerous competitive exams based on their career aspirations. Some of the most popular competitive exams include:

a) Civil Services Examination (UPSC)

Conducted by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC), this exam is one of the most prestigious in India. It opens doors to administrative roles such as IAS, IPS, and IFS. The exam consists of three stages: Preliminary, Mains, and Interview.

b) SSC CGL (Staff Selection Commission Combined Graduate Level)

The SSC CGL exam is conducted for recruitment to various Group B and Group C posts in government ministries, departments, and organizations. It tests candidates on reasoning, quantitative aptitude, and general awareness.

c) Banking Exams (IBPS PO, SBI PO, RBI Grade B)

Banking exams such as IBPS PO, SBI PO, and RBI Grade B recruit candidates for managerial roles in public sector banks and the Reserve Bank of India. These exams evaluate aptitude, reasoning, and English skills.

d) GATE (Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering)

GATE is primarily for students seeking postgraduate admissions in engineering and technology. It is also accepted for recruitment by PSUs (Public Sector Undertakings) like ONGC, IOCL, and NTPC.

e) CAT (Common Admission Test)

CAT is a highly competitive exam for admission into prestigious management programs like MBA at IIMs and other top B-schools. It tests quantitative ability, logical reasoning, and verbal ability.

f) UGC NET (University Grants Commission National Eligibility Test)

UGC NET is conducted for eligibility as Assistant Professors or for Junior Research Fellowships (JRF) in universities and colleges. It includes two papers focusing on teaching and research aptitude as well as subject-specific knowledge.

g) Defence Exams (CDS, AFCAT, NDA)

Defence-related exams like CDS (Combined Defence Services) and AFCAT (Air Force Common Admission Test) recruit candidates into the Indian Armed Forces. These exams test reasoning, general knowledge, and physical fitness.

h) State Public Service Commission Exams

State PSC exams recruit candidates for administrative and managerial roles within individual states. These exams include general studies, aptitude tests, and state-specific topics.

3. Benefits of Competitive Exams

Competitive exams after graduation help candidates secure prestigious job roles and ensure career growth. Some of the benefits include:

  1. Opportunity to work in government and public sector organizations.
  2. Access to high-paying, secure, and reputed jobs.
  3. Enhancement of skills such as analytical reasoning, problem-solving, and decision-making.
  4. Recognition and respect in society.
4. Preparation Tips for Competitive Exams
  • Create a study plan and stick to it consistently.
  • Understand the exam syllabus and pattern thoroughly.
  • Practice mock tests and previous year question papers.
  • Focus on time management and accuracy.
  • Stay updated with current affairs and general knowledge.
